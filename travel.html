Kerid Crater in Iceland

Geographical Location: North Europe

Kerid Crater, also known as Kerid or Kerith, is a stunning volcanic crater located in Iceland. It is one of the most easily accessible and visually captivating volcanic features in the country. What makes Kerid particularly unique is its vibrant aquamarine-colored lake that fills the caldera, creating a striking contrast with the surrounding red volcanic rock.

The crater is approximately 3,000 years old and is part of the Golden Circle, a popular tourist route in Iceland. Visitors can explore the rim of the crater, take in the breathtaking views, and even descend to the water's edge to fully appreciate the geological wonder that is Kerid Crater. Strookur in Iceland

Geographical Location: North Europe

Strokkur is a famous geothermal geyser located in Iceland, renowned for its frequent and dramatic eruptions. It's situated in the Haukadalur geothermal area, part of the Golden Circle tourist route. Strokkur erupts approximately every 5 to 10 minutes, shooting a column of scalding hot water and steam up to 30 meters (98 feet) into the air. This regular and predictable display makes it a popular attraction for visitors to Iceland.

Tourists often gather around its bubbling pool to witness the thrilling spectacle and capture photos or videos of the eruptions. Strokkur is a testament to Iceland's unique geological features and is a must-see for anyone exploring the country's natural wonders. You can check more information on Guide to Iceland.

Seljalandsfoss in Iceland

Geographical Location: North Europe

Seljalandsfoss is a famous waterfall in southern Iceland known for its unique feature: visitors can walk behind the cascading water curtain. This 60-meter tall waterfall is a popular stop along the Ring Road, offering a breathtaking and immersive experience, especially during the summer months when the surrounding landscape is lush and vibrant. Black Sand Beach in Iceland

Geographical Location: North Europe

The Black Sand Beach in Iceland, also known as Reynisfjara, is a striking natural wonder along the southern coast. Its unique feature is the jet-black volcanic sand, contrasting with dramatic basalt columns and sea stacks. Caution is advised due to powerful waves. This iconic spot, with its petrified troll sea stacks, offers a captivating glimpse into Iceland's rugged coastal beauty. You can check more information on Guide to Iceland.
